Color Overview

In order for a website to be accessible to all people regardless of ability, you must follow these color guidelines.


Color Usage Level A

Do not use color as the only method of distinguishing content or webpage elements.

When displaying links, don't only use color to distinguish links from surrounding text. This can be ignored if the contrast ratio between the link's color and surrounding text is at least 3:1 and an additional distinction (bold or underlined, for example) is used when hovering over the link.


Text ContrastLevel AA

An example of contrast and contrast ratio.

Text or images of text must have a contrast ratio to their background of at least 4.51:1. For text larger than 18pt or 14pt and bold, the contrast ratio must be 3:1.

Enhanced ContrastLevel AAA

Text or images of text must have a contrast ratio of 7:1 or higher. For text larger than 18pt or 14pt and bold, the contrast ratio must be at least 4.5:1.


Element ContrastLevel AA

A representation of contrast for a button.

Contrast between HTML objects or elements (such as icons, buttons, elements of graphs or charts) should be 3:1 or higher.

For interactive graphic elements such as buttons, there must be a 3:1 contrast ration between their states (hover, focus, active, etc.)
