Content Navigation Overview

Follow these guidelines to ensure that your page's navigation is accessible.


Skip BlocksLevel A

Provide a link or button to skip past page elements or navigation that is repeated across web pages.

Properly setting up your heading structure or indeitifying page regions can be considered sufficient.


Page TitleLevel A

Your web page should have a descriptive, informative page title.


Focus OrderLevel A

When focusing, the order of links or other interactive page elements makes sense.


Link PurposeLevel A

A link's purpose can be understood from its text alone, or from its text and surrounding text/context.

Enhanced Link PurposeLevel AAA

A link's purpose is determined from its text only. Additionally, the link does not share text with another link that goes to a different location.


Multiple Methods of NavigationLevel AA

Web pages can be reached through multiple different ways. This includes at least two of

  • A list of related pages
  • Table of contents
  • Site map
  • Site search
  • List of all available pages

Headings and LabelsLevel AA

Headings and labels for forms and interactive elements should be informative.

Enhanced HeadingsLevel AAA

All individual sections of content on a page are identified with headings.


Indicating LocationLevel AAA

If your site contains complex sequences of pages or a page exists inside of a complex site structure, provide an indication of the user's current location. An example of this would be a breadcrumb navigation menu.
